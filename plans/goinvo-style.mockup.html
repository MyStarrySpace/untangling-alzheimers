1906
Alois Alzheimer describes "a peculiar disease"
At a psychiatry conference, Alzheimer presents his findings on plaques and tangles. The audience, eager to hear the next talk on "compulsive masturbation," sends him away without questions.
1992
Hardy & Higgins publish the Amyloid Cascade Hypothesis
A one-page Science paper proposes amyloid deposition as the initiating event in AD. Written in "possibly a week," it would dominate research for 30+ years.
1993
de la Torre proposes the vascular hypothesis
Research shows chronic brain hypoperfusion can initiate neurodegeneration. The field largely ignores it.
2012
Bapineuzumab fails Phase 3 trials
The first anti-amyloid antibody successfully clears amyloid but shows no cognitive benefit. The field's response: "We're treating too late."
